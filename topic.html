What is Astronomy?

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial bodies such as stars, planets, comets, and galaxies. It delves into the origins, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe. From observing the night sky with the naked eye to using advanced telescopes and space missions, astronomy has expanded our understanding of the cosmos.

Why is Astronomy Important?

  • Understanding Our Place: Astronomy helps us understand our place in the vast universe.
  • Technological Advancements: Research in astronomy has led to many technological developments used in everyday life.
  • Curiosity and Exploration: It satisfies our innate curiosity about the unknown and drives exploration.
  • Environmental Awareness: Studying planets and their atmospheres can give insights into climate change on Earth.

Fascinating Facts About Our Cosmos

  1. The Milky Way galaxy is just one of billions of galaxies in the universe.
  2. Black holes are regions in space where gravity pulls so much that even light can't escape.
  3. Neutron stars can spin at a rate of 600 rotations per second.
  4. The observable universe is about 93 billion light-years in diameter.
